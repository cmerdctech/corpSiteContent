Transition Skills for Students on the Autism Spectrum


Sixty-three transition skills are identified for students whose behavior is on the Autism Spectrum. These 63 skills are grouped into four categories: Verbal and Nonverbal Communication, Social Interaction, Restrictive and Repetitive Patterns of Behavior, and Miscellaneous Skills

1. Advocating for Self
2. Listening Well
3. Paying Attention to Speaker
4. Following Directions Promptly
5. Following Verbal Directions Well
6. Following Written Instructions or Procedures Well
7. Comprehending Written Information Well
8. Writing Clearly and to the Point
9. Conversing Effectively on the Telephone
10. Speaking Appropriately in the Workplace
11. Presenting Ideas in a Clear and Logical Manner
12. Promoting Own Ideas Effectively and Appropriately
