▾ Planning Ninja

• An elaborate note-keeping tool for people who want to get stuff done

• Based on the idea of shared goals and buy-in

• The opposite of micro-management

▾ Ideas are a Dime a Dozen

• They flit out of existence as soon as they pass

• They only exist when observed (Calling Mr Schroedinger!)

• That piece of napkin will be at the bottom of a pile, lost forever, in two days

• But if you text the words on it or a picture of it to your Planning Ninja Management Space…

▾ The Distance between Idea and Action is a Goal

• Maybe the idea is a goal

• Maybe it’s ten

• If you don’t write them down, they never be fulfilled

▾ Doing What You Do

• Do a Ben Franklin, crank up a little spreadsheet

• Take a few notes, ask a few questions

• Figure out if an idea is worth pursuing

• In a place where they will be easy to find

▾ For a Planning Ninja

• The first step is to establish goals

• The second is to enlist collaborators

• The third is to let go

▾ The Opposite of Micro-Management

• Goals are not assignments. Not a list of tasks.

• A goal is an opportunity for collaborators to join the effort and apply their creative energy to accomplishment

• For collaborators, a goal is a new idea that needs to be explored.

• Each collaborator is a Planning Ninja, too

▾ Introducing The Achievement Manager

• A tool designed by Planning Ninjas, for Planning Ninjas

• Easy to use, comprehensive, conceptually solid

• Goals are identified and shared; collaborators buy-in

• Every goal is supported by a Management Space shared by collaborators

• Collaborators make new goals and share them with new collaborators

▾ Comprehensive Tool to Keep Track of Accomplishment

• Explain how you will know if a goal is successfully fulfilled

• Identify accountable activities (who, when, money, success)

• Review collaboration and results in the context of goals.

• Provide visibility to collaborators, stockholders and the public

• Support ongoing efforts by duplicating and editing plans to make new plans
